optimizes DC V offset, as well as the phase/gain correction. Before calling this function, make sure that the microwave sources/spectrum analyzer are well configured/connected (sweep time[BW], lvo amplitude, frequency range) SPECIAL CARE: CHANGING qua.IO1 via set_IO1 will change the amplitude of the pulse!!!

Parameters

  • fints_ghz – intermediate frequencies in GHz

  • sweep_time_s – sweep time in s so that the measurement doesn’t happen too fast

  • report_progress – if defined, will be used as callback to report on progress

  • dc_range – typically 0.145

  • remove_multiplier – which band to remove. by default -1 to remove -fint_hz

  • resize_window – resize_window(fint_hz) resizes the mxa window so all 3 frequencies lvo(+/-fint_hz) are observable

  • measure_spectrum_analyzer_power – …(fint_hz) customized callback for measures the power on the marker at flo+fint_hz, returns dBm

optimise_qb_readout(op_readout_res: str, fint_scan_GHz: numpy.ndarray, df_GHz: float, meas_pulse_length_ns=4000, save_verif: str = False, SNR=30)¶

Here we determine the electrical delay after measurement of the resonator spectrum over the range defined by f_scan_GHz and remove it to have a correct phase behavior. We then determine the required measurement time N * meas_pulse_length_ns that allows to detect a shift of the resonator df_GHz with a given SNR (in amplitude).

The function returns:

  • the total measurement time = N * meas_pulse_length_ns, calculated to detect a shift of the resonator df_GHz with the required SNR

  • and a tuple (compl_spectrum, compl0, compl1, compl2) containing

    1. compl_spectrum is the des-embedded resonator spectrum around resonance in the (I,Q) complex plane

    2. compl0 are complex values of the des-embedded spectrum when the measurement frequency is tuned at resonance frequency showing the convergence of the measurement as the number of accumulations is increased up to 1000 accumulations.

    3. compl1 are complex values of the des-embedded spectrum when the measurement frequency is tuned at resonance frequency showing the convergence of the measurement as the number of accumulations is increased up to N. N*meas_pulse_length_ns is calculated to detect a shift of the resonator df_GHz with the required SNR

    4. compl2 are complex values of the des-embedded spectrum when the measurement frequency is detuned from resonance frequency by df_GHz (fres-df) showing the convergence of the measurement as the number of accumulations is increased up to N. N*meas_pulse_length_ns is calculated to detect a shift of the resonator df_GHz with the required SNR

To save the values on the hard-disk, you can use save_verif = True (default location of quicksave ) or set save_verif to the string path

Parameters

  • save_verif – specify the path string if you wish to save the verification results

  • df_GHz – the resonator shift that you wish to detect, supposing we are measuring at f_center

  • op_readout_res – the readout operation string

  • fint_scan_GHz – the frequency range over which the resonance will be detected

  • meas_pulse_length_ns – readout pulse duration, should be ~ T1

Returns

total measurement time in ns to to detect a shift of the resonator df_GHz with the required SNR , and a tuple containing the compl verification results mentionned hereinabove

Return type

Tuple[float,Tuple[np.ndarray]]
